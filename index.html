A letter for you

From the heart

Dear Mumma,

There are words I have always meant to say- on ordinary Tuesdays, in the quiet of the kitchen, or in those soft moments between school runs and lesson plans. Today, they do.

You have spent a lifetime shaping minds. As a computer teacher, you showed your students not just how to navigate a screen, but how to think clearly, patiently, and with purpose. And at home, you did the very same for me, guiding me through every difficult problem with the steadiness of someone who knows that every error has a solution.

Thank you for being my first teacher, my greatest comfort, and my favourite person (the one I tease and yap with the most).

With all the love I have- always 🤍

A few favourite moments

Memories I treasure

The paper checking days

Watching you grade papers late into the night, pen in hand, taking every student seriously- teaching me what dedication really looks like.

Sunday mornings

The smell of your cooking filling every corner of the house, those mornings felt like the entire world was perfectly still.

The hard days

Every time I stumbled, you simply said: "Try again. Don't trust anyone but yourself" Words that have now changed everything for me.

Every ordinary day

The daily rhythms, the quiet routines, the small kindnesses repeated a thousand times, this is the love that built me.

"A good teacher reaches a hundred students.
A good mother shapes an entire life."
This exactly describes you.
